Cocktail Recipes

Many of these cocktails are classics, with many variations and therefore no specific inventor. Some we have created, or adapted from others' creations: the provinence of each cocktail is recorded, cryptically, next to the name. If the original recipe is available online, we have provided an active link.

Due to copyright restrictions, the actual recipes for cocktails taken from published books are not provided, only the basic ingredients and approximate proportions. For a list of references, go here.

If no other instructions are given, just mix the ingredients and serve, shaken or not, or over ice or not, with soda or not: as you wish.

Remember that alcohols can be substituted for slight variations in flavour, or if you don't have the exact brand.

  • Gran Marnier, Cointreau, Triple Sec, Mandarin Napoleon, and Orange Curacao are all orange liqueurs/brandies.
  • Absinthe, pernod, ouzo, and pastis are all liquorice brandies; sambuca is similar but much sweeter.
  • Whisky differs by location and blend—Irish, Scotch, Canadian (rye), American (bourbon)—but they can be substituted effectively.
  • etc.

  • Limoncello: 0.5
  • Lemon juice: 0.5
  • Cranberry juice: 0.5

  3. Shake quickly over ice (in shaker or not).
  4. Strain well and serve in a cold martini glass.

Grilled Paloma

Ingredients
  • Grapefruit: 1
  • Limes: 2
  • Agave syrup: 0.25
  • Grapefruit juice: 2 (from fruit above)
  • Lime juice: 1 (from fruit above)
  • Tequila: 2
  • Club soda: 1 (optional)
Instructions
  1. Cut the grapefruit and limes in half and grill for 4-5 minutes, until beginning to blacken on the edges.
  2. Shake all ingredients (except soda) together over ice.
  3. Serve in glasses with salted rims.
  4. Add soda if you want.

J

Jasmine Syrup

Ingredients
  • 250 ml boiling water
  • 250 ml white sugar
  • 2 tbsp jasmine tea leaves
Instructions
  • Pour boiling water over tea and sugar in a cup.
  • Stir until sugar dissolves.
  • Let steep for 15 minutes.
  • Strain, and store sealed in the refrigerator.

  • Maple syrup: 0.25
  • Angostura bitters
  • Maraschino cherry
Instructions

  • The original recipe does not call for the maraschino cherry, but our friend makes them at home and the addition of the homemade cheery is spectacular. Not so sure what it would be like with commercial cherries, although most Old Fashioned recipes call for them.
